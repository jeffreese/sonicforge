# Music Theory Principles

Core guidelines for generating musical compositions that sound good.

## Voice Leading

- Move between chords with smallest possible note movements
- Keep common tones between chords (C major → A minor shares C and E)
- Bass moves by step, fourth, or fifth. Inner voices move by step or hold.

## Energy Arc

- Add instruments gradually: intro sparse → verse moderate → chorus full
- Increase velocity across sections: verse mf → chorus f → climax ff
- Higher register and rhythmic density for climactic moments
- Outro: thin instrumentation, lower velocity, resolve to tonic

## Velocity and Expression

- Emphasize downbeats (+5–10 velocity over off-beats)
- Melodic peaks louder, passing tones softer
- Ghost notes on drums at velocity 30–40 between main hits
- Never write all notes at the same velocity — sounds mechanical

## Rhythm

- Drums anchor: kick on 1 and 3, snare on 2 and 4 (in 4/4)
- Bass locks with kick drum rhythmically
- Hihat steady 8ths or 16ths with velocity variation
- Syncopation on some notes for interest

## Register Separation

- **Bass:** C1–G3 (mostly C2–E3)
- **Piano/Guitar comping:** C3–C5
- **Melody:** C4–C6
- **Pad/Strings:** C3–G5
- Don't stack multiple instruments in the same octave range

## Arrangement

- Sparse (intro/outro): 1–2 instruments
- Medium (verse): 2–4 instruments
- Full (chorus): 3–6 instruments
- Leave space — not everything at maximum density simultaneously
